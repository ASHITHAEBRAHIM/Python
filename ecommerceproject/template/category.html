Jewels, also known as gemstones or precious stones, are naturally occurring minerals or organic materials that possess beauty, rarity, durability, and often, great value. Ruby is one of the four precious gemstones, and its red color is caused by the presence of chromium. Emerald: A green gemstone, a variety of the mineral beryl, and valued for its vivid green color and rarity. Amethyst: A purple variety of quartz, amethyst is a popular gemstone for jewelry due to its attractive color and availability. Topaz: A gemstone with a wide range of colors, including yellow, blue, pink, and more. It is often used as a substitute for more expensive gems. Opal: Known for its unique play of color, opal is a hydrated silica mineral with a captivating appearance. Pearl: An organic gemstone formed inside the shells of certain mollusks, primarily oysters and mussels. Pearls are valued for their luster and beauty.
